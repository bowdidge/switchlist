Example Layout: Chicago Belt Lines

This layout represents a Chicago-area switching layout containing trackage for two belt lines: the Belt Railway of Chicago (BRC) and Baltimore and Ohio Chicago Terminal (B&OCT).

This layout is based on the layout plan in "Moving Freight around Chicago in N scale", which appeared in the November 2012 Model Railroader magazine.

The BRC runs from Clearing Yard to the 16th Street Yard near the Western Electric Hawthorne Works, and on to an interchange with the B&OCT I informally named "Chicago Jct." The B&OCT, running slightly farther outside of Chicago, runs from Barr Yard through Robey Yard, and meets the BRC at Chicago Jct. On the actual layout, Barr and Clearing Yards share a single two-ended staging yard.

Key details to check out on this layout:

  • Note how Chicago Jct's interchange track is set up to allow both railroads to exchange cars with each other.
  • Watch how cars go from the Santa Fe interchange on the B&OCT, through the interchange, and on to Western Electric.
  • Note that both major railroad interchanges (Chicago and North Western and Santa Fe) both are set up as yards so empty cars will be returned to the interchange tracks.
  • For both of the intermediate yards (Robey and 16th Street), separate towns were used for the yard and for the nearby industries. This will make it easier to do transfer runs from staging to the yards, and have a separate yard job switching nearby industries.
